Nallur Kandaswamy Kovil

Nallur, Jaffna town

The spiritual heart of Jaffna — a magnificent Dravidian temple dedicated to Lord Murugan with a golden gopuram visible for miles. Men must remove shirts to enter. No cameras inside.

4:15 AM – 6:15 PM Free entry ↗ Map
📚

Jaffna Public Library

Clock Tower Road, Jaffna

One of Asia's finest libraries and a proud symbol of Tamil heritage. Rebuilt after 1981, it stands today with stunning colonial architecture and a peaceful garden.

Tue–Sun, 8:30 AM – 6 PM Closed Mondays ↗ Map
🏛

Mantri Manai

Near Nallur, Jaffna

The ancient residence of King Sangiliyan's chief minister — a rare surviving relic of Jaffna's royal past with atmospheric 16th-century stone architecture.

Near Nallur Kovil ↗ Map
🍦

Rio Ice Cream

AB20, Near Nallur Kovil

A Jaffna institution. Famous for the Rio Special, 5-flavour Jumbo, and handmade coconut fudge. Pay at the counter first, then collect. Always crowded — a good sign!

Daily 9 AM – 10 PM Budget-friendly ↗ Map
🏰

Jaffna Fort

Lagoon waterfront, Jaffna

A vast 17th-century Dutch colonial fortification overlooking the Jaffna lagoon. Walk the broad ramparts, visit the old church inside, and enjoy sweeping water views.

Jaffna town centre ↗ Map
🌊

Keerimalai Sacred Spring

Kankesanthurai, ~15 km north

A geological wonder — freshwater spring flowing just metres from the saltwater sea. Sacred to Hindus and perfect for sunset watching. Separate pools for men and women.

8 AM – 7 PM daily Best at sunset ↗ Map
☸️

Nagadeepa Temple

Nainativu Island — boat required

One of Sri Lanka's most revered Buddhist shrines, marking Lord Buddha's second recorded visit. A 20-min boat ride from Kurikadduwan Jetty makes the journey itself memorable.

9 AM – 4:30 PM Boat Rs. 80–100 ↗ Map
🛕

Nainativu Nagapooshani Amman Kovil

Nainativu Island — same trip

An ornate Hindu temple dedicated to Goddess Nagapooshani on the same island as Nagadeepa. Free vegetarian meals are offered to all visitors around 1 PM daily.

6 AM – 6 PM Free lunch ~1 PM Boat required ↗ Map
🏖

Casuarina Beach

Karainagar, ~25 km from Jaffna

Jaffna's most popular beach — shallow crystal-clear water safe for all ages, shaded by casuarina trees. Calm year-round, ideal for families. Vehicle entry fee applies.

~25 km away Family-friendly ↗ Map
🧭

Point Pedro

Northernmost tip of Sri Lanka

Stand at the very top of Sri Lanka — the island's northernmost point. A lighthouse, fishing village, and open sea stretching toward India. A landmark every visitor should see.

~40 km from Jaffna ↗ Map
☸️

Dambakola Patuna Viharaya

Northwest coast, near Casuarina

Sacred landing site where Sanghamitta Thero arrived from India in the 3rd century BCE with the Bo tree sapling. A serene coral beach sits directly behind the temple.

9 AM – 6 PM Near Casuarina Beach ↗ Map

Before You Go

Planning Tips

🚢
Island day trip

Nagadeepa and Nainativu Kovil are on the same island. Combine them in one day. Take Route 776 bus from Jaffna to Kurikadduwan Jetty. Last boat back by 5 PM.

🌅
Northwest coast day

Casuarina Beach and Dambakola Patuna are near each other. Add Keerimalai Spring and time arrival for sunset — one of the most memorable views in the north.

🏙
Jaffna town cluster

Nallur Kovil, Mantri Manai, Rio Ice Cream, and the Public Library are all within a short drive. Ideal for a single morning or afternoon exploring.

👕
Temple dress code

At Hindu temples, men must remove shirts. Both must cover knees. No footwear or cameras inside. Sarongs available near Nallur Kovil entrance.

🕐
Best time to visit

January to April is ideal — dry weather, clear skies. Avoid visiting during Nallur Festival (July–August) unless you plan to join the 25-day celebration.

🚌
Getting around

Tuk-tuks are the easiest way for short distances. For beaches and outer sites, rent a motorbike or hire a private vehicle for the day — very affordable.
